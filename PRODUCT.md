# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective clients ranging from early-stage startups needing their first brand and product, to growth-stage companies looking to level up their digital presence, to established businesses ready for a serious refresh. They arrive having already done some research — they're evaluating whether Kagency is the right creative partner, not whether they need an agency at all. They're sitting at a desk, laptop open, comparing two or three studios. They want to feel like Kagency gets them before a single conversation happens.

## Product Purpose

Kagency is a full-service creative studio offering brand identity, design, development, and strategy under one roof. The site exists to convert a skeptical, research-mode visitor into a confident prospect who reaches out. Success means the portfolio does the heavy lifting — the work speaks, the copy confirms, and the form gets filled.

## Brand Personality

Cool, Invested, Practical.

Not the loudest agency in the room — the one that was already thinking about your problem before you finished the sentence. Expert confidence without arrogance. Warm but never sentimental. The kind of studio that says "we probably know better what you need than you do" and earns the right to say it through the work.

Voice: Direct, slightly dry, never corporate. Sentences end when the thought ends. No throat-clearing.

## Anti-references

- The generic "we craft digital experiences" agency template: hero metric tiles, gradient text, glassmorphism cards, identical icon grids.
- Over-produced award-site aesthetics (Awwwards bait): scrolljack for its own sake, 3D blobs, parallax that slows navigation.
- Cold, minimal studios that feel like design firms with nothing to say.
- Any site where you could swap the logo and not notice.

## Design Principles

1. **The work is the hero.** Every layout decision either showcases the work or gets out of the way. Never compete with it.
2. **Confidence, not decoration.** The red accent earns its keep by being rare. Restraint IS the brand statement.
3. **Invested warmth.** Editorial rhythm, handwritten moments, and copy that sounds like a person — not a pitch deck.
4. **Practical directness.** No intros that restate the heading. No section that exists to fill space. Every element justifies its weight.
5. **Cool is earned.** Personality comes from precision and timing, not effects. The one surprising detail lands harder than ten.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No hard WCAG level requirement. Apply good practice throughout: sufficient contrast, focus states, reduced-motion support, meaningful alt text on all portfolio imagery.
